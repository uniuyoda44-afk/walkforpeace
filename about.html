Purpose of the Walk for Peace

Approximately two dozen Buddhist monks, led by Bhikkhu Pannakara, will embark on a 2,300-mile Walk for Peace from Fort Worth to Washington, D.C., beginning October 26, 2025, to promote national healing, unity, and compassion.

Rooted in both spiritual devotion and civic purpose, the Walk for Peace seeks to remind Americans that peace is not a destination—it is a practice.

“We walk not to protest, but to awaken the peace that already lives within each of us. The Walk for Peace is a simple yet meaningful reminder that unity and kindness begin within each of us and can radiate outward to families, communities, and society as a whole.”

Aloka: The Peaceful Walk Dog

As the monks and walkers journey through towns and along rural highways, their presence draws curiosity, kindness, and sometimes silent reverence from onlookers. Among the group, one gentle soul has captured hearts both on the road and online: Aloka, the peaceful walk dog.

Aloka, a medium-sized dog with an alert yet gentle presence, walks when he can, rests when he needs to, and often leads the way. His calm demeanor mirrors the monks’ intention, making him feel less like a mascot and more like a fellow pilgrim. Aloka’s journey is followed by many on social media, where his quiet companionship and resilience inspire people far beyond the roadside.

In the words of Rev. Nguyen, vice president of the temple, the walk is “a journey to bring blessings to countless lives in a world filled with uncertainty and restlessness.” Aloka’s steady steps remind us all of the power of presence, peace, and compassion—one paw at a time.

Legacy & Vision

The Walk for Peace is more than a journey—it is a living legacy of compassion, unity, and hope. Our vision is to inspire future generations to walk the path of peace, to build bridges across cultures, and to honor the sacred teachings of the Buddha through service and love.

We invite you to join us in this ongoing mission, to carry the light of peace into your own life and community, and to help create a world where all beings may be happy, safe, and free.

More About the Walk for Peace

The Walk for Peace is a unique pilgrimage that brings together monks from diverse Buddhist traditions, lay supporters, and peace advocates. The journey is not only a test of physical endurance but also a profound spiritual practice, emphasizing mindfulness, compassion, and the interconnectedness of all beings.

Each day, the monks rise before dawn, gather for meditation and chanting, and then set out on foot, walking in silence and harmony. Along the way, they visit temples, schools, and community centers, sharing teachings of peace and hope. The walk is supported by volunteers who provide food, shelter, and encouragement, creating a moving community of kindness.

The Dhammacetiya Stupa, featured in our journey, is a symbol of the Buddha’s teachings and a beacon of spiritual inspiration. Stupas are sacred monuments that enshrine relics and scriptures, serving as places of pilgrimage and meditation for Buddhists worldwide. The presence of the stupa on this walk reminds us of the timeless values of wisdom, compassion, and peace.

Through this walk, we hope to inspire individuals and communities to cultivate peace within themselves and extend it to the world. The impact of the Walk for Peace is felt not only in the miles traveled but in the hearts touched, the friendships formed, and the seeds of harmony sown along the way.

  • Daily meditation and chanting sessions open to the public
  • Visits to historic temples and cultural landmarks
  • Community service projects and interfaith dialogues
  • Workshops on mindfulness, non-violence, and compassion
  • Opportunities for volunteers to join and support the walk
